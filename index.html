Privacy & Data

Subscriptions & Purchases
Free: up to 3 accounts, 3 budgets, 3 recurring transactions, 1 portfolio ticker (24-hour price refresh), and 5 AI Chat prompts per day.

Pro (one-time SGD 3.98): unlimited accounts, budgets, and recurring transactions. iCloud backup and CSV export unlocked. Same AI Chat and portfolio limits as Free.

Premium (SGD 3.98/month): everything in Pro, plus 200 AI Chat prompts per day, unlimited portfolio tickers, and faster portfolio price refreshes (every 10 minutes).
No. Pro is a one-time purchase of SGD 3.98. You pay once and keep it forever on the Apple ID you purchased with. Only Premium is a monthly subscription.
Pro includes the same 5 AI Chat prompts per day that Free users get. To unlock 200 AI Chat prompts per day and other AI-related benefits, you need Premium.
Open Settings inside the app and tap Upgrade. The purchase is processed by Apple using your Apple ID. Pro users who later want Premium can upgrade from the same screen.
Premium is managed through Apple. Open your iPhone’s Settings, tap your name at the top, tap Subscriptions, and select Equator Premium to cancel. You will continue to have access until the end of the current billing period. We cannot cancel subscriptions on your behalf.
All refunds are handled by Apple, not by us. Visit reportaproblem.apple.com, sign in with your Apple ID, find the purchase, and tap Request a Refund. We do not have the ability to issue refunds directly.
Install Equator on the new device, open Settings in the app, and tap Restore Purchases. Make sure you’re signed in with the same Apple ID you used to buy Pro or subscribe to Premium. Your data does not transfer automatically — use an iCloud backup to move transactions across devices.

You simply lose access to Premium-only limits — the 200 AI Chat prompts per day reverts to 5, unlimited portfolio tickers reverts to 1, and the 10-minute refresh reverts to 24 hours. None of your existing data is deleted, hidden, or locked. Resubscribing restores the Premium limits immediately.
Your existing data stays fully accessible and functional. Using the App
Equator supports seven account types: Cash, Bank Account, Savings Account, Investment Account, Prepaid Account, Debit Card (linked to a parent account), and Credit Card (with billing cycle tracking).
Yes. Each account can be set to its own currency, and you can enable multi-currency mode on a single account to hold balances in several currencies at once (useful for travel wallets or multi-currency savings accounts). The app converts balances to your home currency for overall summaries.
Equator supports a wide range of global currencies for your accounts and transactions. Exchange rates are provided for your home currency plus 17 major world currencies. You can set your home currency in Settings → General.
Go to Settings → General and select your home currency. All summary totals (net worth, spending, budgets) will be recalculated using live exchange rates. Your individual transactions stay in the currency they were recorded in.
When you create a Credit Card account, you can set the billing cycle day (the day each month your statement closes) and the payment due days (how many days after cycle close the payment is due). Equator will automatically track your current cycle spend, previous cycle spend, and due dates. This data is all computed locally — we don’t connect to your bank.
When you create a transaction, you can mark it as recurring with a frequency of Daily, Weekly, Monthly, or Yearly. Equator tracks all instances in the recurring series as a group so you can edit or delete them together. You can manage recurring transactions from Settings → Recurring.
Yes. When you create a transaction, you can mark it as an installment purchase and specify the total number of installments and interest rate. Equator will track your progress through the installment plan and can help you see your monthly commitment.

Backups & Data Management
Backup is available with Pro or Premium. Go to Settings → Data & Backup and tap Export Backup to save a JSON file to your iCloud Drive. Pro and Premium users can also enable automatic backups with configurable frequency. Your device’s standard iOS/iCloud backup also includes Equator’s data automatically, separately from this in-app backup.
Go to Settings → Data & Backup and tap Import Backup. Select the JSON file from your iCloud Drive. The app will replace your current data with the contents of the backup, so make sure you’re restoring the right file.
The easiest way is to export a backup on your old phone, install Equator on the new phone, sign in with the same Apple ID, restore your purchases, and import the backup. Alternatively, if you’ve restored your new phone from an iCloud backup of your old phone, Equator’s data should already be there.
Yes, with Pro or Premium. Go to Settings → Data & Backup and tap Export to CSV. You can export transaction history or portfolio data. CSV files are standard spreadsheet format and can be opened in Numbers, Excel, or Google Sheets.

AI Chat
AI Chat (“Eqqie AI Assistant”) is an in-app chatbot that can answer questions about your own financial data. Ask it things like “How much did I spend on food last month?” or “What’s my biggest expense category?” and it will analyse your transactions to give you an answer. Responses are generated by Google’s Gemini AI.
When you send a message in AI Chat, your transactions, accounts, budgets, and portfolio data from the last 6 months are transmitted to our server and forwarded to Google’s Gemini API to generate a response. We do not send your name, email, Apple ID, IP address, or device identifier to Google. Our server does not store your data. For full details, see Section 1.7 of the Privacy Policy.
Free and Pro users can send up to 5 AI Chat messages per day. Premium users can send up to 200 per day. The count resets every 24 hours.
Each AI Chat message costs us real money to send to Google’s Gemini API. The daily limits keep the service sustainable as a solo developer. The Premium tier at SGD 3.98/month allows us to offer a much higher limit to users who rely on the feature.
Go to Settings → Privacy and toggle off “Enable AI Chat.” This hides the AI Chat feature entirely and ensures no financial data will be sent off your device through AI Chat. You can re-enable it anytime.
No. AI Chat answers are for informational purposes only. Like any large language model, it can make mistakes — it might miscategorise a transaction, misread an amount, confuse income with expense, or draw incorrect conclusions. We are not licensed financial advisors. Always verify what AI Chat tells you against your actual records before making financial decisions.
AI Chat responses are generated by Google’s Gemini model, which is a general-purpose language model and not a deterministic calculator. It can misinterpret transactions, miscount amounts, or miss subtleties in your notes. Treat AI Chat as a smart helper that occasionally makes mistakes, not as a source of truth. For exact numbers, check your transaction list, account balances, or the built-in reports directly.
Within a single conversation, yes — the app sends the last 20 messages with each new request so Google’s model has context. Chat history is stored locally on your device and can be cleared at any time from Settings → Data & Backup. Our server does not store any chat history.
Troubleshooting
First, check your internet connection. Then force-close the app and reopen it. Free and Pro users see prices refresh every 24 hours; Premium users refresh every 10 minutes. If prices still don’t appear, the third-party market data service may be temporarily unavailable. Email us if the issue persists.
Double-check the investment transactions you’ve entered: the number of shares, average cost, and currency. P&L is calculated as current market value minus invested amount. If exchange rates haven’t refreshed recently, totals in your home currency may look off briefly. If you still think there’s a bug, email us with a screenshot.
If you’ve hit your daily limit (5 for Free/Pro, 200 for Premium), wait 24 hours for it to reset. If AI Chat returns errors despite having quota remaining, check your internet connection or try again in a few minutes — the AI service may be temporarily overloaded. Email us if it persists.
Check that you’re signed in with the correct Apple ID, iCloud Drive is enabled on your device, and you have enough iCloud storage. Backups are saved to the Equator folder in iCloud Drive — you can browse to that folder in the Files app to verify the files exist. If the backup file is there but the app can’t see it, wait a few minutes for iCloud sync, then try again.
Go to Settings inside the app and tap Restore Purchases. Make sure you’re signed in with the same Apple ID you used when buying Pro. If the restore doesn’t work, check your purchase history at reportaproblem.apple.com to confirm the charge went through, then email us with your Apple receipt and we’ll help.
Try force-closing the app and reopening it. If the issue persists, restart your device. If it still happens, email us with a description of what you were doing when it crashed and, if possible, a screenshot. Enabling Apple’s iOS analytics (Settings → Privacy & Security → Analytics & Improvements → Share with App Developers) helps us see crash reports if one occurs.
